What Are Smart Appliances?

Smart appliances bring advanced technology into your kitchen and laundry room, helping you manage everyday tasks with a tap on your phone or a voice command to your smart speaker. These devices are connected to your home’s Wi-Fi and are often integrated with apps like Samsung SmartThings, LG ThinQ, Whirlpool’s connected platform, or Amazon Alexa/Google Assistant. From setting cooking timers on your oven remotely to receiving alerts when your washer finishes a cycle, smart appliances offer unmatched convenience.

Common Types of Smart Appliances

Today’s smart appliances are more than just trendy—they’re practical tools that make everyday chores simpler and more efficient. Here are some of the most common smart appliances seen in Auburn homes:

Smart Refrigerators

Modern refrigerators do more than keep food cold. Many feature touch screens, built-in cameras to view contents remotely, and app-connected grocery lists. When software problems arise, such as freezing firmware, camera malfunctions, or connectivity loss, professional repair service is often needed.

Smart Washers & Dryers

Wi-Fi-enabled washers and dryers allow you to monitor cycles, start loads remotely, and receive maintenance alerts. Problems such as failed remote start, app syncing issues, or faulty sensors can interrupt operation but can be diagnosed and repaired by trained technicians.

Smart Dishwashers

Advanced dishwashers can auto-order detergent, send cycle notifications, and alert you when cleaning is complete. Connectivity issues, app registration errors, or sensor malfunctions may affect performance, but these problems can be professionally repaired.

Smart Ovens & Ranges

Control your oven and range from your smartphone, receive preheat notifications, or sync cooking settings with online recipes. Smart cooking appliances make meal preparation easier, but touchscreen glitches, inaccurate app commands, or connection failures may require expert repair attention.
