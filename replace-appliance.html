Appliance Lifespan: What to Expect

Every appliance has an expected lifespan. If your unit is nearing or past this range, it might be more cost-effective to replace it—especially if it needs frequent service or major parts. Here’s a general rule of thumb for average appliance lifespans:

Refrigerators: 10—15 years

Ovens & Ranges: 13—16 years

Dishwashers: 8—12 years

Washers & Dryers: 10—13 years

Microwaves: 7—10 years (Note: We do not service microwaves)

If your appliance is older than the typical lifespan and experiencing problems, a new model may be a better investment.

Signs It Might Be Time to Replace

Not sure whether to schedule another repair? Here are signs it might be time to shop for something new:

Frequent breakdowns or recurring issues

Appliance is making loud, unusual noises

Excessive energy usage or rising utility bills

Performance has noticeably declined

Replacement parts are expensive or hard to find

Cost of repair is more than 50% of the cost of a new unit

All appliance repair companies always do their best to repair your appliance affordably—but if it no longer makes sense to keep fixing it, they’ll let you know.

Benefits of Replacing an Outdated Appliance

Improved Efficiency: New appliances use less energy and water—saving money over time.

Modern Features: Enjoy smart technology, better design, and improved safety features.

Fewer Repairs: A new appliance brings peace of mind without the worry of ongoing issues.

Increased Home Value: Upgraded appliances can boost your kitchen or laundry room appeal.
