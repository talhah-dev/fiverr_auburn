Common Stove & Oven Problems Repaired

Oven or Stove Won’t Turn On

If your stove or oven isn’t powering up, the problem could be as simple as a tripped breaker or as complex as a faulty control board or igniter. Local stove repair experts will quickly diagnose the issue and provide an effective solution.

Burners or Oven Not Heating Properly

Are your burners failing to heat or your oven cooking unevenly? This can be caused by a broken heating element, defective thermostat, or faulty infinite switch. The technician that shows up will test all components and make the necessary repairs to ensure consistent cooking temperatures.

Gas Stove Igniter Not Working

A gas stove that clicks but doesn’t ignite is often due to a clogged gas line, worn-out spark electrode, or malfunctioning safety valve. A local technician will inspect, clean, and repair these components to restore proper gas flow and ignition.

Oven Temperature Fluctuations

An inaccurate oven temperature can lead to undercooked or overcooked meals. This issue is usually caused by a faulty temperature sensor, broken thermostat, or failing control board. The oven repair expert that comes out will calibrate your oven’s temperature and replace any defective parts to ensure precision cooking.

Oven Door Won’t Open or Close Properly

If your oven door won’t open after a self-cleaning cycle, it may be due to a faulty door latch. On the other hand, if it won’t close tightly, heat may be escaping, causing inefficient cooking. All local oven repair experts repair and replace hinges, gaskets, and latch mechanisms for a secure fit.

Strange Smells or Gas Odors

Unusual smells coming from your stove or oven could indicate gas leaks, burning wires, or grease buildup. If you detect a gas leak, turn off your appliance immediately, exit your house, call 911 and also call for urgent repair service. Safety is top priority.
