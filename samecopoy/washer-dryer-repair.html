Common Washing Machine Problems Can Be Fixed

Your washing machine is essential for keeping your clothes clean and fresh. If it’s not working correctly, it can be frustrating and costly. The washing machine repair techs that come out to your house handle all major washing machine problems, including:

Washer Won’t Start

If your washing machine won’t turn on, check the power supply first. If the issue persists, the problem may be a faulty door latch, broken control board, blown fuse, or a defective timer. A professional washing machine repair technician can quickly diagnose the issue and restore your washer’s functionality.

Loud Noises or Strange Sounds

Is your washing machine making loud banging noises? This could be due to a damaged drum bearing, worn-out shock absorbers, or a faulty motor mount. Unusual noises could also indicate that small objects (like coins) are stuck inside the drum. Don’t ignore these warning signs—get a repair expert to inspect your washer before the problem worsens.

Washer Won’t Spin

If your washing machine won’t spin, your clothes will come out soaking wet. This issue can be caused by a broken lid switch, worn-out drive belt, faulty motor coupling, or a damaged transmission. A professional repair tech will pinpoint the cause and provide a lasting solution.

Leaking Washing Machine

A leaking washer can cause serious water damage to your home. Common culprits include a cracked water inlet valve, a worn-out door seal, or a clogged drain hose. A professional technician will inspect your machine and fix any leaks before they lead to mold growth or flooring damage.

Washer Won’t Drain

If your washing machine won’t drain, it might be due to a clogged drain hose, faulty drain pump, or a jammed water pump filter. Ignoring this issue can lead to water pooling in your laundry area. A repair expert will remove blockages and ensure proper drainage.

Dryer Not Heating

Is your dryer running but not heating? This is one of the most common dryer repair issues. It could be caused by a faulty heating element, a blown thermal fuse, a broken thermostat, or a clogged dryer vent. A technician can diagnose and repair the issue so your clothes dry efficiently again.

Dryer Won’t Start

If your dryer won’t turn on, it may be due to a blown fuse, defective start switch, or a broken drive motor. Before calling for repairs, make sure your dryer is plugged in and that your circuit breaker hasn’t tripped. If the problem persists, a repair technician will troubleshoot and fix it.

Loud Noises or Strange Sounds

A noisy dryer can be caused by a worn-out drum bearing, loose blower wheel, or damaged drum rollers. These components need to be replaced to ensure your dryer runs smoothly and quietly.

Dryer Takes Too Long to Dry Clothes

If your dryer is running but your clothes stay damp, you could have a clogged vent, a failing heating coil, or a faulty cycling thermostat. A technician will clean out lint buildup and inspect all components to ensure your dryer works efficiently.

Dryer Won’t Tumble

If your dryer drum isn’t spinning, it may be due to a broken drive belt, defective idler pulley, or a worn-out motor. A professional repair expert can replace these parts and get your dryer working again.
