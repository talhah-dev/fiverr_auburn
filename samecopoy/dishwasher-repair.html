Common Dishwasher Problems We Fix

Dishwasher Making Loud or Unusual Noises

A dishwasher should operate quietly, running in the background as it cleans your dishes. If your appliance has started making loud grinding, humming, or rattling sounds, it's time for a professional inspection. Unusual noises can indicate a damaged motor, worn-out pump housing, loose spray arms, or a faulty wash impeller. Ignoring these sounds may lead to further damage, requiring more costly repairs. An Auburn dishwasher repair technician can quickly diagnose and resolve the issue.

Water Leaking from the Dishwasher

A leaking dishwasher isn't just inconvenient—it can lead to water damage, mold growth, and expensive repairs. The most common causes of dishwasher leaks include:

  • Worn-out door gaskets that no longer provide a tight seal
  • Faulty water inlet valves that cause excess water buildup
  • A broken door latch that prevents proper closure
  • Cracked hoses or loose connections leading to external leaks

Don't let a minor leak turn into a major problem. An experienced dishwasher repair technician in Auburn will inspect your appliance, identify the source of the leak, and implement a lasting fix.

Dishwasher Won't Start or Power On

If you press the start button and your dishwasher remains unresponsive, you may have a problem with:

  • A faulty control board
  • A blown thermal fuse
  • A broken door switch or latch
  • Electrical issues or a tripped circuit breaker

Before calling for repairs, check if the dishwasher is properly plugged in and ensure the breaker hasn't tripped. If those aren't the cause, an Auburn dishwasher repair expert can assess the unit and restore functionality.

Dishwasher Won't Fill with Water

A dishwasher that won't fill with water won't be able to clean your dishes properly. Potential causes include:

  • A malfunctioning water inlet valve
  • A clogged float switch that prevents water from entering the unit
  • Issues with the electronic control board or door latch

When you call Auburn Appliance Repair Pros, we will connect you with Auburn dishwasher service professionals that will diagnose the problem, clear blockages, and replace any defective parts to restore proper water flow.

Dishwasher Won't Drain

After a wash cycle, your dishwasher should drain completely. If water is left standing at the bottom, you might be dealing with:

  • A clogged drain hose
  • A faulty drain pump motor
  • Blockages in the garbage disposal connection

A dishwasher repair expert in Auburn will clear any obstructions and ensure your dishwasher drains properly to prevent odors and mold growth.

Dishes Aren't Getting Clean

If your dishwasher runs a full cycle but leaves dishes dirty, it isn't performing its job. The issue could be due to:

  • Clogged or malfunctioning spray arms
  • Low water pressure or a failing water pump
  • Food debris buildup preventing proper washing
  • Hard water mineral deposits blocking water flow

Dishwasher Not Drying Dishes

If your dishes come out wet after a full cycle, the problem may be caused by:

  • A broken heating element
  • A malfunctioning high-limit thermostat
  • A faulty rinse aid dispenser

Many modern dishwashers rely on heated drying or condensation drying, and a malfunction in these components can leave your dishes dripping wet. An Auburn dishwasher repair expert can fix the issue so your dishes come out clean, dry, and ready to use.

Detergent Dispenser Not Working

If your dishwasher isn't releasing detergent properly, the unit won't clean your dishes effectively. Common culprits include:

  • A malfunctioning timer or control board
  • A broken bi-metal release
  • A faulty wax motor that prevents proper detergent dispensing

If you notice detergent residue in the dispenser or if your dishes come out greasy, it's time to call an Auburn dishwasher repair specialist.
