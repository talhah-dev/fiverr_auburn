Common Gas Appliances in the Home

Many homes in Auburn still rely on gas appliances for their speed and cost-effectiveness. Here are the most common ones serviced:

Gas Ranges And Ovens

Gas cooking appliances offer instant heat and unmatched temperature control. However, they can run into issues like:

Burners won’t ignite or stay lit

Gas smells or delayed ignition

Oven not heating evenly or at all

Malfunctioning igniters or thermostat failures

Gas Dryers

Gas dryers heat up quickly and dry clothes more efficiently than their electric counterparts. Common problems include:

No heat or long drying times

Burning smell or strange sounds

Ignition problems or faulty gas valves

Lint buildup causing heat blockages

Gas Water Heaters & Other Systems

While not always in the scope of appliance repair, some homes also use gas-powered water heaters and combination units. If a connected gas appliance is showing signs of trouble, it’s always smart to have a professional inspect it for safety and performance.

Gas Appliance Safety Tips

Install Carbon Monoxide Detectors: Place detectors near gas appliances to help protect your home and family from harmful gas exposure.

Never Ignore Gas Smells: If you notice a sulfur or “rotten egg” smell, turn off the appliance, ventilate the area, and seek professional help immediately.

Schedule Professional Maintenance: Regular servicing helps detect leaks, faulty components, and venting issues before they become dangerous.

Keep Vents and Filters Clean: Clean airflow systems improve efficiency while reducing fire risks and unnecessary strain on appliances.

If you ever have concerns about the safety or performance of your gas appliance, don’t wait—have a qualified technician inspect it right away.

When to Call for Gas Appliance Repair

Gas appliances tend to show very clear signs when something’s not right. Here are some red flags that it’s time to call Auburn Appliance Repair:

Your gas burners click but don’t ignite

You notice soot, discoloration, or smoke from burners

You smell gas when the appliance is running—or even when it’s not

The appliance won’t heat or the flame burns yellow instead of blue

Gas appliance problems are never something to “wait and see” with. If something seems off, call right away to avoid dangerous conditions or long-term damage.
