What Makes an Appliance Energy Efficient?

Energy-efficient appliances are designed to do the same jobs as traditional models—but they use less electricity, gas, and water while doing it. Many are certified by the ENERGY STAR® program, a government-backed symbol for energy efficiency that helps consumers make informed choices. Look for the ENERGY STAR label when shopping—it means the appliance meets or exceeds strict efficiency guidelines set by the EPA.

Energy Efficient Appliances By Type

Refrigerators

Energy-efficient refrigerators use improved insulation and advanced compressors to reduce power consumption. Look for models with inverter compressors, LED lighting, and automatic temperature control. Side-by-side and French door models tend to be more efficient when built to ENERGY STAR specs.

Washers & Dryers

High-efficiency washers use less water per cycle and require less detergent. Many front-load models spin faster, extracting more water and shortening drying time. For dryers, look for moisture sensors and low-heat settings. Heat pump dryers, although newer, are exceptionally energy efficient.

Dishwashers

Modern energy-efficient dishwashers use less water and heat and often feature soil sensors that adjust cycles based on how dirty the dishes are. Some also include half-load or eco-modes to conserve energy for smaller loads.

Ovens & Stoves

While ovens and ranges use a significant amount of energy, modern models include better insulation, faster preheat times, and convection settings that cook food more evenly—and faster. Induction cooktops are especially efficient, using magnetic fields to heat cookware directly with minimal energy loss.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Appliances

Clean Filters Regularly: Always clean filters and vents on a routine basis to prevent clogging and improve appliance efficiency.

Avoid Overloading: Do not overload washers or dishwashers as it reduces cleaning performance and shortens appliance lifespan.

Use Energy-Saving Modes: Use eco or energy-efficient settings whenever available for better performance and lower power consumption.

Reduce Heat Usage: Turn off heated dry on dishwashers and air-dry clothes when possible to save energy and improve efficiency.
