Buzzing or Humming Sounds

Some light humming is normal—especially when the compressor kicks on. But if it gets louder or constant, the culprit could be:

A dirty or failing condenser fan motor

A malfunctioning compressor

Loose components vibrating against the back panel

Fix: Vacuum the coils and fan area. If the sound persists, a technician may need to test the motor or compressor.

Clicking or Popping Noises

Occasional clicking is normal during temperature changes or defrost cycles. But rapid or repeated clicking may indicate:

A failing start relay (common before compressor failure)

A faulty thermostat cycling too often

Electrical arcing or control board issues

When to worry: If the fridge stops cooling shortly after clicking, call a professional.

Rattling or Vibrating Sounds

Rattling can come from inside or behind the unit. Common causes include:

Loose drip trays or shelves

Refrigerator touching cabinets or flooring

Water lines or cords vibrating against walls

Quick fix: Level the fridge and secure any loose items or tubing.

Gurgling or Dripping Sounds

These noises usually happen during the defrost cycle or when refrigerant is moving through the cooling lines. While usually harmless, they can become louder if the drain tube is clogged.

Tip: Clear the defrost drain with hot water if water pools inside the fridge or freezer.

Squealing or Screeching

If your fridge sounds like it’s screaming, the evaporator fan motor could be failing. This fan circulates cold air, and when it wears out, it may squeal, grind, or make intermittent high-pitched noises.

Fix: A technician can inspect and replace the fan motor if needed.
