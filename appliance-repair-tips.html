Refrigerator Troubleshooting

Fridge not cooling? First, check the thermostat setting and make sure it hasn’t been bumped. Then, inspect the condenser coils—if they’re dusty, vacuum them carefully for better airflow.

Leaking water? This is often due to a clogged or frozen defrost drain. Try flushing it with warm water if you feel comfortable doing so.

Fridge making loud noises? Pull it away from the wall and check for vibrations. Sometimes it’s just the unit touching the cabinet or a loose panel.

Washer & Dryer Repair Tips

Washer won’t drain? Clean out the drain filter (usually located behind a small panel at the base) and check the hose for blockages.

Washing machine won’t spin? Make sure the load is balanced—an uneven load can stop the spin cycle to prevent damage.

Dryer not heating? Confirm that the lint trap and exterior vent are clean. Poor airflow is one of the most common causes of long drying times.

Dryer won’t start? Check the door switch and confirm that the breaker hasn’t tripped.

Dishwasher Repair Tips

Not cleaning dishes? Run a cleaning cycle with vinegar to clear out hard water buildup and clean the spray arms with a toothpick or pin.

Dishwasher not draining? Check the air gap or garbage disposal connection. Also inspect the filter in the bottom of the tub for food clogs.

Smells bad? Wipe down the rubber gasket, door, and filter area. Then run a cleaning cycle with baking soda and vinegar.

Oven & Stove Repair Tips

Gas burner won’t light? Make sure the igniter is clicking and clean any food debris from around the burner cap and ports.

Electric burner not working? Try swapping the burner with another one of the same size to see if the problem is with the element or the socket.

Oven won’t heat evenly? An oven thermometer can help you check the actual temperature. If it’s consistently off, it may need calibration—or a new thermostat.

Signs Your Electric Appliance May Need Service

Not sure if your appliance is due for repair? Here are some common symptoms to watch for:

Breaker frequently trips when appliance is in use

Appliance doesn’t respond or shuts off mid-cycle

Visible sparks or burning odor near power connection

Unusual noises or performance issues

Electrical components can wear out over time. When that happens, it’s important to get a qualified technician involved to prevent further damage—or potential safety hazards.
